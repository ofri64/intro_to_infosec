1    - In this section we perform a stealth port scanning - using SYN packets 
2     
3    - The reason this type of port scanning is also 
4    called stealth port scanning is that it doesn't 
5    actually complete the 3 ways handshake and doesn't create an established 
6    tcp connection to the attacked host 
7     
8    - In practice the attacker sends SYN and then FIN packet so no TCP 
9     connection/session is established and the attacked host doesn't keep/log 
10    information about this connection. 
11    
12   - The solution is quite simple. 
13   First we craft matching TCP-SYN packets for each port 
14    
15   - Then we inspect our responses: 
16       - SYN request wasn't answered at all - means the port is filtered 
17       - SYN request answered with SYN+ACK response - means the port is 
18       open and ready for connection establishment 
19       - SYN request answered with RST response - 
20       means the host is up, maybe listening on other ports but this specific port is considered close 
21   - Now all that's left is to iterate over our answers 
22    (and packets we didn't get any response for) 
23    and classify depending on the flags in the TCP response.
